Evidence standards

Methodology and corrections

Claimer keeps support, challenge, and context evidence separate and stores source-backed evidence, not editorial conclusions.

Evidence quality is evaluated by source relevance, source type, and whether the source actually supports the submitted evidence summary.

If source coverage looks missing, stale, or incorrect, use the sourced-evidence or feedback path. Corrections mean adding or challenging sourced evidence, not rewriting a claim into an editorial conclusion.
